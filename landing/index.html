JurisLab

A free Microsoft Word add-in fusing STEM authoring and patent & legal drafting — chemistry & structures, mass spec, enzyme kinetics, peptides, statistics, equations, plots, finance, Bluebook citations & Table of Authorities, and more. 20 tools, 100% offline.

New in this release — a life-science suite

🧫 Bio/Assay NewOffline curve fitting in Word — enzyme kinetics (Michaelis–Menten, Hill), dose–response (IC50/EC50, 4PL), and binding (Kd/Bmax), with parameters ± SE, R², and the fitted curve over your data. Plus Cheng–Prusoff, Henderson–Hasselbalch, Beer–Lambert, dilutions & A260/A280 quantitation.
⚛️ Mass Spec NewExact monoisotopic & average mass, the theoretical isotope pattern (the tell-tale ~32% M+2 for chlorine), and common adduct m/z — [M+H]⁺, [M+Na]⁺, [M+2H]²⁺, [M−H]⁻ and more.
🧪 Properties & druglikeness NewFor any structure: cLogP, logS, tPSA, H-bond donors/acceptors, rotatable bonds, plus the Lipinski Rule of Five and Veber screens — capabilities ChemDraw gates behind a license.
🔗 Peptide NewDraw a peptide's 2D structure from its sequence — one-letter (ACDEFG) or three-letter (Ala-Gly-Ser) codes — with formula and molecular weight.
📐 Stats NewDescriptive stats, t-tests (Welch/Student/paired), ANOVA and regression with real p-values and APA reports, plus uncertainty propagation through a formula.
🔬 Online name → structure NewResolve an arbitrary IUPAC name (e.g. 2-amino-3-(1H-indol-3-yl)propanoic acid) via OPSIN — strictly opt-in with a consent prompt; everything else stays fully offline.
🧪 Compound library Updated359 named structures — added 100 large/complex compounds (violacein, morphine, cholesterol, paclitaxel, chlorophyll, heme…), every SMILES sourced from PubChem.

Private by design. Everything runs on your machine — nothing you type leaves your computer. The one exception is the optional online name→structure lookup, which is off until you use it and prompts for consent before sending a name. Works on Word for Windows & macOS (Microsoft 365 / 2019+).

Get it

Download the install pack for your OS from the install folder, unzip, and run the installer (full steps in the install guide). Then open Word → Insert → Add-ins → JurisLab.
