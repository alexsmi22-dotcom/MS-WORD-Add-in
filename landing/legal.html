Why this and not another subscription

One pane, not four vendors

Structures in a drawing package, sequence listings in a separate WIPO tool, citations in a research service, numerals checked by eye. Each is its own login, export and paste-back. This is one pane, in the document you're already writing.

Confidential by construction

Nothing is transmitted, so there's no vendor holding your client's unpublished claims. That isn't a policy promise — the computation happens in the pane.

Free, per-user install, no admin rights

No licence, no seat count, no IT ticket to get it approved.

It flags rather than fixes

The audit reports what looks wrong and leaves the judgement to you. It never silently rewrites your claims.

Drafting aid — not a filing check.

Verify everything before it goes out. ST.26 listings must be validated in the WIPO Sequence tool before filing — JurisLab generates the XML, it doesn't replace WIPO's validator. Numeral and cross-reference detection is advisory: it reads the conventional forms and will not catch every variant. Bluebook output follows the common rules, not every jurisdiction's local quirks.

Get started

Two minutes: unzip, run the installer, fully restart Word (all windows — this is the step people miss), then Insert → Add-ins → Developer Add-ins → JurisLab.
