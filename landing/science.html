Real answers, not decoration

Things you can type today and check against the literature:

toluene → ¹H NMR
δ 7.17 (2H, m), 7.09 (2H, d), 7.08 (1H, t), 2.37 (3H, s, CH₃ on Ph) — literature: 2.34.
y'' = -9.81, z = 0, z' = 20, stop when z
“z reached zero at t = 4.077472” — the closed form is 20/4.905 = 4.0774719673. Type the second-order equation; the reduction is done for you.
Robertson kinetics — rate constants 0.04 → 3×10⁷
Solves in ~570 steps with mass conserved to 12 digits. Stiff kinetics is the normal case whenever rate constants differ by orders of magnitude — an explicit solver simply can't finish it, and JurisLab switches automatically.

Why this and not another tab

One pane, not six applications

The frustrating part of the big suites isn't the science — it's that structures, kinetics, spectra and stats each live in separate software you flip between, then paste screenshots into Word. This is one pane, in the app where the writing already happens.

Nothing leaves your machine

No account, no server, no telemetry. Your compounds, sequences and unpublished data are computed locally and never transmitted. The single exception — an optional IUPAC name lookup — asks every time and sends one chemical name, never your document. That matters if your structures are confidential.

Free, and no seat to renew

No licence, no per-seat cost, no admin rights to install.

It tells you when it doesn't know

An unconjugated molecule reports “no λmax” rather than inventing one. Fused rings say they're approximate. MS fragment “likelihood” is labelled a ranking, not an intensity. You can only trust a tool that admits its edges.

What predicted spectra are — and aren't.

They're estimates from published additivity rules, not measured spectra and not quantum-chemical calculations. Structure recognition is exact; the numbers carry real uncertainty (≈ ±0.2–0.4 ppm for ¹H, ±2–4 ppm for ¹³C). Use them to assign peaks you already have — never as a substitute for running the spectrum. Every screen in the add-in says so too.

Get started

Two minutes: unzip, run the installer, fully restart Word (all windows — this is the step people miss), then Insert → Add-ins → Developer Add-ins → JurisLab.
