Table of Contents

Insert a Table of Contents built from your Heading 1–3 styles, with page references (FRAP 28(a)(2)). Put the cursor where the TOC should go first.

After it runs, select all (Ctrl/⌘+A) and press F9 to fill in page numbers. Section titles must use Word’s Heading styles to appear.

Table of Authorities

Scan the whole document for citations and build a grouped, alphabetized authorities list (Cases · Statutes · Rules · Regulations · Patents · Other) — FRAP 28(a)(3). Unpublished (WL / LEXIS) decisions and Fed. R. Civ. P. rules are included.

Lists every authority with how many times it’s cited, flagging any used more than once — a running record to check before you build the table. Nothing is added to the document. Once you’ve built the field-based table below and pressed F9, Find again to show each authority’s page numbers too.

To build a finished static TOA with page numbers — 3 steps:
1. Click “Insert with live page numbers” (puts in a temporary table).
2. Select all (Ctrl/⌘+A) and press F9 (Word fills the pages).
3. Click “Insert formatted list” — it copies those pages into the final two-line, italic, Times New Roman table and removes the temporary one for you.

Don’t need page numbers? Just click “Insert formatted list” on its own — you get the formatted table with blank page slots to fill. (Page numbers can only be computed by Word’s field + F9; that’s why step 1–2 exist.) Patents appear under “Other Authorities” (Word has no Patents category).

Deletes every hidden TA (citation) field so a malformed or duplicated table can be rebuilt from a clean slate — then run “Insert with page numbers” again. Undo with Ctrl/⌘+Z. Leaves other fields (dates, the TOA itself) alone.

Deletes the generated Table of Contents and Table of Authorities fields (the tables themselves). Pair with “Remove all citation marks” for a full reset. Undo with Ctrl/⌘+Z.

Note: under FRAP 32(f) the Table of Contents and Table of Authorities are excluded from the brief’s word/length count.
