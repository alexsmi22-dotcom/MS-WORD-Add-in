JurisLab

A free Microsoft Word add-in fusing STEM authoring and patent & legal drafting — a no-code numerical workbench (matrix math, optimization, FFT, ODEs, data insights), chemistry & structures, mass spec, enzyme kinetics, peptides, statistics, equations, plots, finance, Bluebook citations & Table of Authorities, and more. 21 tools, 100% offline.

Matrices insert as clean, editable Word tables. 🧮 Analyze — optimization, FFT & ODEs NewMinimize a function (Nelder–Mead) you type; run an FFT on a sampled signal for its amplitude spectrum and dominant frequencies; solve an ODE or system (adaptive RK45) with a sampled table and trajectory plot — all offline. 🧮 Data → insights NewPaste a data table and get per-column summaries, a correlation matrix (Pearson r + p-value, Spearman ρ), trend detection, outlier/missing-data flags, and plain-language actionable insights — computed from your own numbers. 📐 Stats — expanded NewNon-parametric tests (Mann–Whitney U, Wilcoxon signed-rank), chi-square (goodness-of-fit & independence), two-way ANOVA, and multiple-comparison correction (Bonferroni, Holm, Benjamini–Hochberg) — on top of the existing t-tests, ANOVA & regression. 🧪 pKa estimation NewFor any structure, JurisLab detects the ionizable groups (carboxylic acids, amines, phenols, pyridines…) and reports their typical pKa plus an estimated net charge at pH 7.4 — an honest functional-group estimate, computed offline. 🧪 Compound library Updated359 named structures with every SMILES sourced from PubChem — violacein, morphine, cholesterol, paclitaxel, chlorophyll, heme and more.

Private by design. Everything runs on your machine — nothing you type leaves your computer. The one exception is the optional online name→structure lookup, which is off until you use it and prompts for consent before sending a name. Works on Word for Windows & macOS (Microsoft 365 / 2019+).

Get it

Download the install pack for your OS from the install folder, unzip, and run the installer (full steps in the install guide). Then open Word → Insert → Add-ins → JurisLab.
